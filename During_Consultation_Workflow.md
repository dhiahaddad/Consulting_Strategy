## Phase 1: Introduction & Goal Setting (0-10 min)

### Opening (0-2 min)
- [ ] Welcome and establish rapport
- [ ] Start recording (if agreed upon)
- [ ] Confirm time available
  > "We have 45 minutes together. I'll make sure we save the last 5 minutes for wrapping up and next steps."

### Context Review (2-5 min)
- [ ] Confirm you understand their research area
  > "Tell me briefly about your research and how this code fits in."

- [ ] Clarify immediate goals for this session
  > "What would make this session most valuable for you today?"

- [ ] Verify what they've tried already
  > "What have you already attempted?"

- [ ] Assess urgency and constraints
  > "Are there any deadlines or time pressures I should know about?"

### Priority Setting (5-10 min)
- [ ] Summarize their goals
  > "So it sounds like you want to focus on X, Y, and Z. Is that right?"

- [ ] Establish realistic scope
  > "Given our 45 minutes, I suggest we prioritize X first, then move to Y if we have time."

- [ ] Get client agreement
  > "Does that sound good to you?"

- [ ] Set expectation for follow-up if needed
  > "If we don't cover everything, we can schedule a follow-up."

**Quick note**: What's the #1 priority?

## Phase 2: Active Consultation (10-40 min)

### General Principles Throughout
- **Think aloud**: Explain your thought process as you work
- **Ask questions**: Ensure you understand their code and context
- **Teach, don't just fix**: Explain the "why" behind recommendations
- **Check understanding**: Pause regularly for questions
- **Watch the clock**: Check time at 20, 30, and 35-minute marks

### Time Check Points

**At 20 minutes (halfway)**
- [ ] Assess progress on main goal
- [ ] Adjust pace if needed
- [ ] Confirm still on the right track
  > "We're halfway through. Are we addressing what you need?"

**At 30 minutes (10 min left of active work)**
- [ ] Decide if current topic needs wrap-up
- [ ] Determine if you can address secondary goals
- [ ] Start transitioning to conclusions

**At 35-38 minutes (wrap-up imminent)**
- [ ] Begin transitioning to summary
- [ ] Finish current thought but don't start new topics

## Communication Best Practices

### Do's ‚úÖ
- **Use their terminology**: Mirror their research language when possible
- **Explain jargon**: Don't assume knowledge of software terms
- **Show enthusiasm**: Be genuinely interested in their work
- **Be encouraging**: Research coding is hard; validate their efforts
- **Ask permission**: "Can I share my screen?" "Mind if I edit this?"
- **Check in frequently**: "Does this make sense?" "Questions so far?"
- **Use examples**: Show, don't just tell
- **Be honest**: If you don't know, say so and offer to research

### Don'ts ‚ùå
- **Don't overwhelm**: Limit recommendations to what's actionable
- **Don't criticize harshly**: Frame as growth opportunities
- **Don't assume knowledge**: Verify understanding of concepts
- **Don't go over time**: Respect the 45-minute boundary
- **Don't write code for them**: Teach them to do it
- **Don't use complex jargon**: Keep language accessible
- **Don't commit to out-of-scope work**: Maintain boundaries

## Handling Common Situations

### Client is confused/lost
- Slow down
- Use simpler language
- Show concrete examples
- Ask them to explain their understanding
- Try a different analogy

### Running out of time
- Acknowledge it: "We're running short on time"
- Prioritize ruthlessly
- Offer to continue in follow-up
- Ensure they have clear next steps anyway

### Technical issues
- Have backup plan (email code, reschedule if necessary)
- Use chat for code snippets if screen share fails
- Stay calm and patient
- Reschedule if issues can't be resolved quickly

### Client wants to go off-topic
- Acknowledge interest: "That's a great question..."
- Redirect gently: "...but let's stay focused on X for now"
- Offer to address in follow-up or via email
- Document for later discussion

### Issue is beyond your expertise
- Be honest: "This is outside my area of expertise"
- Offer what you can: "But here's what I do know..."
- Suggest resources or other experts
- Follow up with research if appropriate

### Client is passive/not engaged
- Ask open questions
- Invite them to share screen
- Have them do the typing
- Check if the topic is actually their priority
